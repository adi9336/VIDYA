# Acceleration

## Student Lens

Students often think acceleration only means "going faster." The deeper idea is change in velocity over time, which includes speeding up, slowing down, and changing direction.

## Analogy Anchor

Use a bus leaving a stop, then braking near a speed breaker. The feeling in the body tells the student something about change in motion, not just motion itself.

## Tutor Moves

- Open with "Bus start hote hi aur brake lagte hi body ko jhatka kyu feel hota hai?"
- Let the student connect the jolt to change.
- Explain acceleration as change in velocity over time, then show that deceleration is negative acceleration.
- Add that turning in a curve also changes velocity because direction changes.
- Keep the language simple: "speed badli ya direction badli, dono me velocity badli."

## Misconceptions To Watch

- Treating acceleration as speed itself.
- Missing negative acceleration during braking.
- Forgetting that direction change can also create acceleration.

## Quick Check

Ask: "Cycle same speed par gol chakkar me ghoom rahi hai. Acceleration hai ya nahi?"

## Visual Cue

Show a bus with three states: starting, constant motion, braking. Pair that with a circular motion sketch where arrow direction changes around the path.
