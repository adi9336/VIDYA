# Distance vs Displacement

## Student Lens

A student usually feels both words mean "kitna chala." The mental gap is that one idea cares about the whole path, while the other cares only about where you started and where you ended.

## Analogy Anchor

Use a walk from home to the kirana shop and back. The student has moved a lot in terms of path, but if they end where they started, the final change in position is zero.

## Tutor Moves

- Start with "Ghar se dukaan gaye aur wapas aa gaye, kitna chale?"
- Let the student count the path first.
- Ask a second question: "Ab bata, starting point se final point kitna alag hai?"
- Introduce the words only after the student notices the difference between total path and shortest straight change.
- Use "path length" for distance and "shortest straight line from start to end" for displacement.

## Misconceptions To Watch

- Thinking distance and displacement are always equal.
- Thinking displacement cannot be zero if a person moved.
- Mixing "shortest path taken" with "shortest straight line between start and end."

## Quick Check

Ask: "Agar tum park ka ek full round laga ke wahi bench par aa jao, distance aur displacement me kya farq hoga?"

## Visual Cue

Show a curved route between two points plus a single straight arrow from start to end. For the return-home case, show the route but mark the final straight arrow as zero.
