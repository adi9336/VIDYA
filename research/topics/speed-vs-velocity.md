# Speed vs Velocity

## Student Lens

Students often hear both words in sports commentary and assume they mean the same thing. The missing insight is that speed tells how fast, while velocity tells how fast and in which direction.

## Analogy Anchor

Use two students running in a school race corridor. One runs east toward the assembly ground, another runs west at the same rate. Their speed can match even though their velocity differs because direction is different.

## Tutor Moves

- Begin with "Do log 5 meter per second se bhaag rahe hain, ek left aur ek right. Same baat hai kya?"
- Let the student say "same" or "different" and probe why.
- Anchor the idea in direction with left-right, east-west, or toward-away language.
- Only then attach the labels: speed for rate only, velocity for rate with direction.
- Mention that changing direction changes velocity even if the numerical speed stays the same.

## Misconceptions To Watch

- Thinking velocity is just a "harder English word" for speed.
- Ignoring direction in motion descriptions.
- Assuming equal speed means identical motion in every sense.

## Quick Check

Ask: "Bus 40 km/h se chal rahi hai. Agar driver U-turn le le par speed meter wahi rahe, kis quantity me zaroor change aaya?"

## Visual Cue

Show two equal-length arrows with opposite directions and the same numeric label. Add a note that the speed value matches while the direction label changes the velocity.
